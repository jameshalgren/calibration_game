Structural & warm-state choices — held out iThese four fix the model structure and its starting (warm) state. The auto-tuner never touches them — so if a structural knob is OFF or a warm state is wrong, no amount of slider tuning recovers the truth. That's the calibration trap.
Elevation bands (low/mid/high)
OFF = uniform melt — the missing knob
Aspect (N/S slopes) iA pure degree-day model has no explicit energy-flux term — so how can aspect matter? Here aspect is applied as a melt-rate multiplier: north-facing slopes × 0.4 (slow, retained late), south-facing × 1.4 (fast, early). That's a lumped stand-in for differential solar exposure. The resulting two-timescale melt shape is exactly what a single uniform melt rate can't reproduce — which is why toggling aspect moves the NSE.
OFF = no solar split — the missing knob
Snow — temperature-index (tunable) iDegree-day model: melt = DDF·(T−T₀) when T>T₀. Elevation bands and aspect (above) shape where and how fast the pack melts; these two sliders are what the auto-tuner searches.
Soil — two-bucket iRoot zone handles infiltration, ET, and redistribution; deep groundwater handles baseflow recession. Rain+melt that can't infiltrate becomes quick runoff.
